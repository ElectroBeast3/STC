Dear Sir hears a story of ours,

Once upon a time, a group of friends were driving together on a long, boring road trip. The sun was setting, the car was packed tight, and the air inside was already a little warm and stuffy. Everyone was chatting, laughing, and arguing over what music to play—until Shabbir suddenly went quiet.

Out of nowhere, he let out a fart.

At first, there was silence. Nobody reacted. It was one of those moments where everyone pretends nothing happened. But then, slowly… the smell began to spread.

One by one, the passengers started to notice.

“Wait… what is that smell?” someone asked, wrinkling their nose.

Within seconds, the entire car was filled with a stench so powerful it felt like it had a physical presence. It was like something had died, come back to life, and died again just to make it worse.

“SHABBIR!” everyone shouted at once.

People started rolling down the windows in panic, gasping for fresh air. One person leaned halfway out of the car, another covered their face with their shirt, and someone in the back was dramatically acting like they were about to pass out.

“I can’t breathe!” one of them yelled.

“It’s toxic!” another cried.

The driver, struggling to keep control while laughing and coughing at the same time, swerved slightly before regaining focus. The car became pure chaos—shouting, coughing, laughing, and accusing all at once.

Meanwhile, Shabbir just sat there, trying not to laugh, but completely failing.

“I didn’t think it would be that bad!” he said, barely holding it together.

“THAT BAD?! We almost died!” someone yelled back.

Eventually, with all the windows open and the car speeding forward like it was trying to outrun the smell itself, fresh air finally started to clear things out. Everyone slowly recovered, though the trauma of that moment would never truly leave them.

From that day on, whenever they went on a trip, there was one unspoken rule:

Shabbir was not allowed to fart in the car.
